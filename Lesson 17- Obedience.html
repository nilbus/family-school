Attention Activity
 

Give each child the papers with the coded messages on them that you have prepared.

This message will give you instructions on how to find something you like. To decode this message, you have to obey the instructions exactly. Erase or cross out every other letter of the message starting with the first letter of each word. When you have figured out the message, please don’t say anything until everyone else has had a chance to figure it out.

When the children are done, the message will look like this: “L O O K I N T H E B O X O N T H E T A B L E”

  • Do you know where to look for the surprise? (Yes).

All of you go together to find it. Allow the children time to get the box and bring back the contents.

  • How were you able to find the surprise? (We followed the instructions exactly, just like you told us to.)

Your obedience to my instructions led you to a fun surprise. (Adapted from Primary 6, Lesson 35, pp. 154–57.)

Lesson
 

Academic Concept: Laura’s and Charley’s disobedience diminishes or lessens their usefulness.

Academic Concept: Laura’s and Charley’s disobedience diminishes or lessens their usefulness.

Research
 

In our reading this week, we found two examples of disobedience.

  • Do you remember what they are? (Yes. Laura hit Mary, and Charley refused to help in the harvest. He didn’t help and he made things harder for Pa and Uncle Henry to harvest the oats.)

Let’s look at each of these events. Read, in “Summertime,” the incident about Laura and Mary, starting with the words: “Aunt Lotty had gone, and Laura and Mary were tired and cross.” End with the words: “But she was glad that Mary had had to gather all the chips.”

  • Why does Laura hit Mary? (Mary says her golden hair is prettier than Laura’s brown hair.)
  • Are those words kind? (No. They are mean.)
  • So was it okay for Laura to hit Mary? (No. Just because Mary was unkind doesn’t mean Laura should hit.)
  • What is Laura not able to do because she was disobedient? (She can’t gather chips for her family.)
  • What does Pa do after he punishes Laura? (He puts Laura on his lap and hugs her. He allows her to tell him what happened.)
  • How does Laura feel when she is in Pa’s lap? (“Everything was all right again.”)
  • If we disobey, is it right for parents to give us consequences or punish us? (Yes.)

Let’s read what Heavenly Father said about consequences when we do wrong. Turn in the Doctrine and Covenants to section 121, verses 41–44:

No power or influence can or ought to be maintained by virtue of the priesthood, only by persuasion, by long-suffering, by gentleness and meekness, and by love unfeigned;

By kindness, and pure knowledge, which shall greatly enlarge the soul without hypocrisy, and without guile—

Reproving betimes with sharpness, when moved upon by the Holy Ghost; and then showing forth afterwards an increase of love toward him whom thou hast reproved, lest he esteem thee to be his enemy;

That he may know that thy faithfulness is stronger than the cords of death.

  • How well does Pa follow these instructions? (He reproves when he needs to, then he shows more love, just like the scripture says.)

Now let’s look at the second case of disobedience.

  • What does Charley do that is wrong?
    • He gets in the way, so Pa and Uncle Henry can’t swing the cradles.
    • He hides the whetstone, so they have to hunt for it when their blades need to be sharpened.
    • He doesn’t bring the water jug quickly. He follows Pa and Uncle Henry around and asks questions.
    • After they tell him to go away, he screams and yells as if he’s been hurt. They leave their work to help him. He does this three times.
  • Why is it important that Pa and Uncle Henry finish their work? (All the oats have to be harvested and put in shocks before rain or dew comes, or it will all spoil.)
  • Why don’t Pa and Uncle Henry go to Charley when he yells the fourth time? (They think he is trying to trick them again.)
  • What happens to Charley? (He is stung all over by yellow jackets.)

That was not a very fun consequence.

  • What was Charley not able to do because of his disobedience? (He couldn’t help his father with the harvest. He was unable to help at all until he was healed of the yellow jacket stings.)

When we are disobedient we experience negative consequences. One of those consequences is that we are not able to do the good we might have done if we had been obedient. Our joy is not as full when we cannot help.

As you see, “Laura’s and Charley’s disobedience diminishes or lessens their usefulness.”

Reason
 

Gospel Principle: Obedience is an essential part of Heavenly Father’s plan.

  • Why is it important to be obedient? (When we obey we don’t get in trouble. We don’t have to be punished. We receive blessings for obedience.)
  • Why does Heavenly Father want us to obey? Answers may include the following:
    • So we won’t get hurt. To help us be safe.
    • So we can have the Spirit with us.
    • So we can go back to live with Him again.
    • So He can send us more blessings.
    • It shows our love and gratitude.
    • We are more able to bless the lives of others.

Being obedient is part of Heavenly Father’s plan. When we are obedient, we become more like Heavenly Father and become more ready to join His kingdom.

Elder Patrick Kearon gave a talk in general conference about obedience. He told a story about his childhood that helps us understand why obedience is so important and what might lead us to disobey. Let’s watch a video of his story.

Watch (1)   The Sting of the Scorpion.

  • What reason did Elder Kearon give for his disobedience? (He said he was both lazy and a little rebellious.)
  • Were Laura and Charley either lazy or rebellious? (Yes. They both were. Laura was upset with Mary and wanted to hurt her, something she knew was wrong. Charley didn’t want to work; he was lazy. He was also rebellious. He wanted to make things hard for his father.)

Relate
 

Like Laura and Charley, we too can be “stung by the scorpion” when we are disobedient.

Look at the paragraph we just read.

  • In what ways does Elder Kearon say we justify disobedience? Let’s underline what he says with our red pens.

  • We’re not really doing anything wrong.
  • It doesn’t really matter.
  • Nothing all that bad will happen.
  • Everyone else is doing it and some are doing even worse things.
  • We think we are the exception to the rule, so we won’t experience the consequences.

  • What commandments and family rules are easy for you to obey? (Answers will vary.)
  • What commandments and family rules are hard for you to obey? (Answers will vary.)

Look at the last paragraph of the story again and the ways we justify our disobedience.

  • Do you have any of these kinds of thoughts when you choose to do wrong? (Yes.)

This is called justifying or making excuses for wrong choices. It is easier to choose obedience if we recognize the ways we justify our bad behavior. Later on, in our record assignments, you will be able to choose one way you are often disobedient and plan a way to overcome that tendency. As we become more obedient, we will be able to do our part to fulfill Heavenly Father’s plan for us.

“Obedience is an essential part of Heavenly Father’s plan.”
